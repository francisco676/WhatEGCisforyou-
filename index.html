Which Employee Content Play Is Right for Your Company?

A 3-minute framework for B2B teams who want LinkedIn to actually drive business results.

3 minute read

Most employee advocacy programs fail for the same reason.

They hand employees pre-written copy, set up a Slack channel called #linkedin-posts, and wait. Two weeks later, nobody's posting. Six weeks later, the initiative is dead.

The problem isn't that employees don't want to build their presence. The problem is that the system puts all the effort on them and gives them nothing in return except the vague promise of "visibility."

The companies winning on LinkedIn right now aren't running campaigns. They're building systems that make it easy and genuinely rewarding for employees to show up as experts. And there are three distinct ways to do it, depending on what your company needs most.

1
For Founders, CEOs & Senior Executives
Brand Leadership
The problem
Your executive team has years of hard-earned expertise and relationships. But their LinkedIn hasn't been touched in months, or when they do post, it sounds like a press release. They know they should show up, but there's no time and no system.
What it looks like
A structured monthly interview, 15 minutes per person. We turn that conversation into 4-6 posts written in their voice, reviewed and approved by them, published on their schedule. They show up as genuine experts without the overhead of actually writing.
What happens
Inbound from ideal customers Speaking invitations Investor credibility Company brand halo effect
Best fit if
Founders and C-suite leaders who know presence matters but haven't found a way to make it sustainable.
2
For Marketing Managers, Comms Leads & Brand Teams
Brand Awareness
The problem
Your marketing team runs the company page and does a decent job. But no ad budget replicates the trust that 15 or 20 employees talking naturally about their work creates. The challenge isn't buy-in, it's bandwidth. Chasing that many people for content every week is unsustainable.
What it looks like
We build and run a structured employee content program across your team. Interviews, writing personalized to each person's voice, an editorial process that keeps it on-brand, and a posting cadence that doesn't burn anyone out.
What happens
Compound organic reach Employees proud of their presence Brand that feels human Scales as the team grows
Best fit if
Marketing teams who want their people active on LinkedIn but need someone to make it operationally possible.
3
For Sales Leaders, VP Sales & RevOps
Lead Generation
The problem
Cold outreach is getting harder and reply rates keep falling. But an SDR with a warm, credible LinkedIn presence gets responses that a cold rep never will. The issue is most salespeople don't know how to build that presence, and they don't have time to figure it out while hitting quota.
What it looks like
A content engine built specifically for your sales team. Posts that showcase expertise, create social proof, and warm up the exact accounts they're targeting before the first touch ever happens.
What happens
Higher outbound reply rates Prospects recognize reps before first call Credible team, less effort
Best fit if
Sales teams where LinkedIn is already part of the outreach motion but not delivering the results it should.

Quick comparison

Brand Leadership
1-5 executives. Monthly interview. Thought leadership content. Fast to monetize.
Brand Awareness
10-30 employees. Team-wide program. Compound organic reach. Marketing-led.
Lead Generation
SDR and sales teams. Pipeline-focused content. Warms outbound before first touch.
